# Xinca Bracelet Creation Tutorial :

[TOC]

## Cultural inspiration:

The Xinca are an indigenous people of southeastern Guatemala, near the border with El Salvador. Their name may be linked to the Nahuatl word "Tzinaka", meaning "bat", a symbol often associated with protection and intuition in indigenous cultures. This tutorial will guide you in creating a bracelet inspired by Xinca culture, with bat motifs and natural materials like wood, reflecting their deep connection with nature.

## Numerical version (with picture)

Make a Tinkecad Xinca-inspired bracelet. Bellow there a physical version with wood [here](#IRL Version (without picture)).

#### Virtual materials:

- Basic shapes in Tinkercad: cylinders, spheres, custom shapes.

---

#### Step-by-step instructions:

##### 1. Open Tinkercad:

- Log on to [Tinkercad](https://www.tinkercad.com) and click on "Create new design" to start an empty project.

---

##### 2. Create the Bracelet Base:

- Drag a **cylinder** from the shape toolbar onto the work surface.
- Resize it using the handles:
  - Diameter: 60 mm (standard wrist size).
  - Thickness: 5 mm.
- Add a second cylinder, resize it to 55 mm diameter, and define it as a "Hole" (toolbar option).
- Align the two cylinders at the center, then use the "Group" tool (Ctrl+G) to subtract the hole and form a hollow strip.
  *Image description: A 60 mm solid cylinder with a 55 mm "Hole" cylinder superimposed, followed by a view of the hollow band after grouping *.

![Image description: A 60 mm solid cylinder with a 55 mm "Hole" cylinder superimposed, followed by a view of the hollow band after grouping.](./assets/img/Capture%20d%27%C3%A9cran%202025-02-24%20183353-1740421264906-11.png)

![Image description: A 60 mm solid cylinder with a 55 mm "Hole" cylinder superimposed, followed by a view of the hollow band after grouping.](./assets/img/Capture%20d%27%C3%A9cran%202025-02-24%20183416-1740421253711-9.png)

---

##### 3. Model a Regular Pearl:

- Drag a **sphere** onto the work surface.
- Adjust its size to 5 mm in diameter using the resizing handles.
- Duplicate the sphere (Ctrl+D) 10 to 15 times to create a set of beads.

![Image description: A single 5 mm sphere, then a row of duplicated spheres aligned on the work surface.](./assets/img/Capture%20d%27%C3%A9cran%202025-02-24%20183657.png)

![Image description: A single 5 mm sphere, then a row of duplicated spheres aligned on the work surface.](./assets/img/Capture%20d%27%C3%A9cran%202025-02-24%20183917.png)

---

##### 4. Create a Bat Pattern:

- Select the "Scribble" tool to draw a bat silhouette:
  - Draw a small circle for the body (approx. 3 mm).
  - Add two curved shapes on either side for the wings.
- Once the design is complete, select complete and extrude it in 3D to a thickness of 5 mm.
- Add a small cylinder (2 mm in diameter) defined as a "Hole" in the center to allow a cord to pass through, then group the elements together.
*Image description: A bat silhouette drawn in the Scribble tool, then extruded in 3D with a central hole visible.

---

##### 5. Arrange Beads on Bracelet:

- Move the spherical beads and bat motifs onto the hollow strip.
- Use the "Align" tool (icon in the top bar) to arrange them evenly around the bracelet.
- Create a balanced pattern by alternating 2 or 3 regular beads with a bat.

![Image description: Several spherical beads and bat motifs aligned around the hollow band, showing a regular pattern.](./assets/img/Capture%20d%27%C3%A9cran%202025-02-24%20190602.png)

---

##### 6. Finalize the design:

- Select all shapes (band, beads, patterns) and group them with Ctrl+G to make a single object.
- Change colors using the Tinkercad palette: opt for a natural brown to imitate wood, for example. Like the upper picture.

---

#### Tips:
- Use the 3D view (rotate with mouse) to check that beads and motifs are aligned.
- If you wish to manufacture the bracelet, export your design as an STL file via the "Export" button for 3D printing.

---

With this tutorial, you can now design a virtual Xinca-inspired bracelet in Tinkercad, step by step, with simulated visual descriptions to guide you. Have fun creating your own piece of jewelry in 3D!

### Materials required:

 - Small pieces of wood (e.g. balsa, easy to carve)
 - Regular wooden beads (prefabricated or handmade)
 - Leather cord or strap (approx. 20 cm long, 2 mm thick)
 - Carving tools (knife, small drill or punch)
 - Paint or natural dyes (optional, for decoration)
 - Scissors
 - Glue (optional, to secure knots)

### Step-by-step instructions :

#### Carve the Bat Shape Bead:
 - Draw a simple bat silhouette on paper (about 1 cm wide).
 - Choose a small piece of wood and carve it to match this shape, about 5 mm thick.
 - Drill a hole in the center with a drill or punch to allow the bead to pass through, then sand the edges to smooth them.
#### Prepare Regular Beads:
 - If you're using prefabricated beads, check that they're uniform (5-8 mm in diameter) and already drilled.
 - Otherwise, carve small spherical beads from wood and drill them. Prepare 10-15 regular beads.
#### Plan the motif:
 - Lay your beads flat to test the design: alternate 2-3 regular beads with a bat-shaped bead. Aim for a total length of around 17 cm (adjust according to your wrist).
#### String the beads:
 - Cut the cord to the desired length, adding an extra 2 cm for the knots.
 - String the pearls onto the cord in your pattern, pulling them tight to keep them in place.
 - Tie a small knot at one end to secure the beads (add a drop of glue if necessary).
#### Add the Closure:
 - Bring the two ends of the cord together and tie a secure knot, or attach a small clasp (such as a metal hook) for ease of use.
 - Trim the excess cord and make sure the knot is discreet or reinforced with glue.
#### Decorate (Optional):
 - Paint the beads with natural dyes: use dark colors (black, brown) for the bats and earthy tones for the other beads, echoing the Xinca environment.
 - Possibly add feathers or other small elements to enrich the cultural style.

### Practical advice:

 - Try the bracelet on your wrist for comfort, and adjust the number of beads if necessary.
 - Work in natural light to choose colors that evoke Guatemala's vibrant landscapes.

This tutorial offers a practical and respectful approach to creating a Xinca-inspired bracelet.
